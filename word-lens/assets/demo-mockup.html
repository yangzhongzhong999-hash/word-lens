Smartphones and Modern Life

Over the past two decades, mobile technology has become ubiquitous in everyday life. People rely on their phones for communication, navigation, and entertainment, often without thinking twice about how dependent they have become.

Researchers note that this shift happened faster than almost any previous technological change in human history, reshaping habits, relationships, and even the way we think.
